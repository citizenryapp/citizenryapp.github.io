Key Differences Explained

Mock Interviews: Citizenry's Exclusive Feature

This is the most significant difference between the two apps. The US citizenship test is an oral interview, not a written exam. A USCIS officer will ask you questions, and you must answer verbally. There are no multiple-choice options, so you need to know the answers and speak them clearly.

Citizenry offers unlimited mock interviews with virtual USCIS agents. These interviews simulate the real experience:

  • A virtual agent asks you questions just like in the real interview
  • Speech recognition evaluates your spoken answers
  • Practice as many times as you want with no judgment
  • Build confidence before your real interview
  • Reduce nervousness on interview day

Citizen Now does not offer mock interviews. You can study the content, but you won't get the practice of actually speaking your answers to a virtual officer. For many applicants, this hands-on practice is what makes the difference between passing and failing.

Ad-Free Experience

Studying for your citizenship test requires focus. Ads can be distracting and frustrating, especially when you're trying to concentrate on memorizing important information.

  • Citizenry: Completely ad-free. No banner ads, no video ads, no interruptions. Ever.
  • Citizen Now: Contains ads throughout the app. You must pay to remove them.

What Both Apps Do Well

Both Citizenry and Citizen Now offer solid study materials for the citizenship test:

  • Complete coverage of both 2008 (100 questions) and 2025 (128 questions) civics tests
  • Flashcards and practice quizzes
  • Audio learning options
  • ZIP code lookup for local representatives
  • High app store ratings

Who Should Choose Each App?

Choose Citizenry if you:

  • Want to practice speaking your answers before the real interview
  • Feel nervous about the oral interview format
  • Prefer an ad-free study experience
  • Want speech recognition to check your pronunciation
  • Study in multiple languages (20+ supported)

Choose Citizen Now if you:

  • Don't mind studying with ads
  • Don't need interview practice
  • Prefer their specific app interface

The Bottom Line

For most people preparing for the US citizenship test, Citizenry is the better choice. The mock interview feature alone is worth it. Practicing speaking your answers to a virtual USCIS agent builds the confidence you need to pass your real interview. Add in the completely ad-free experience, and Citizenry offers a more focused, effective study experience.

Remember: The citizenship test isn't multiple choice. You need to speak your answers clearly to a USCIS officer. Citizenry is the only app that lets you practice exactly that.
