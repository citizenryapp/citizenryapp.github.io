After submitting your N-400 application, you'll want to track its progress. Here's how to check your case status and what each update means.

How to Check Your Status Online

  1. Go to uscis.gov/casestatus
  2. Enter your 13-character receipt number (found on your I-797C notice)
  3. Click 'Check Status'

Common Status Updates Explained

Case Was Received

USCIS has received your N-400 application and it's in the queue for processing. This is the first status you'll see after filing.

Fingerprint Fee Was Received

USCIS has processed your biometrics fee. You should receive a biometrics appointment notice soon.

Case Is Being Actively Reviewed

A USCIS officer is reviewing your application. This is a good sign - your case is moving forward.

Interview Was Scheduled

Your citizenship interview has been scheduled! You'll receive a notice with the date, time, and location. Now is the time to intensify your test preparation.

Interview Was Completed

You've completed your interview. If you passed, your case will soon update to 'Case Was Approved.'

Case Was Approved

Congratulations! Your application has been approved. You'll receive information about your oath ceremony.

Oath Ceremony Was Scheduled

The final step! Once you complete the oath ceremony, you'll officially be a US citizen.

Typical Timeline

Processing times vary by location, but here's a general timeline:

  • Application to biometrics: 2-4 weeks
  • Biometrics to interview: 2-12 months (varies greatly)
  • Interview to oath ceremony: 1-6 weeks

What to Do While You Wait

Use this time wisely! Start studying for your citizenship test as soon as you submit your application. The more prepared you are, the more confident you'll feel at your interview.
