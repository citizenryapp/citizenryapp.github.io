How to Prepare for the N-400 Interview

November 3, 2025

The N-400 interview is a crucial part of your journey to US citizenship. During this interview, a USCIS officer will review your application, verify your identity, and test your knowledge of US civics and English. Here's how to prepare and what to expect.

What Happens During the N-400 Interview?

The interview typically lasts 20-30 minutes and includes several parts:

  1. Identity verification - The officer checks your documents
  2. N-400 review - Questions about your application
  3. English test - Reading and writing sentences
  4. Civics test - Up to 10 questions for the 2008 test or up to 20 questions for the 2025 test about US history and government

Common N-400 Interview Questions

The officer will ask questions based on your N-400 form, including:

  • Your full name and any previous names
  • Your current and previous addresses
  • Your travel history outside the US
  • Your employment history
  • Questions about your moral character

Tips for Success

  1. Review your N-400 application thoroughly before the interview
  2. Bring all required documents organized and in order
  3. Practice speaking clearly and confidently
  4. Answer questions honestly and directly
  5. Don't be afraid to ask for clarification if needed
